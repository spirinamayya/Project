Nutrition Facts for McDonald's Menu


This project’s purpose was to consider nutrition value of different categories of McDonald's Menu. 9 menu sections, 260 menu items were included in the analysis and compared by the contamination of various substances such as nutritional supplements, fat, cholesterol etc.

Regarding to the statistics, it can be seen that due to high sugar content smoothies & shakes are the most high-calorie products (540). Containing a lot of fat, beef & pork, chicken & fish and breakfasts follow smoothies & shakes by calory contamination, 500, 480, 470, respectively.

From the plots it can be seen that categories are presented disproportionately. There are 95 Coffee&Tea items and only 6 salads in the menu. Part of saturated fat in total fat is growing proportionally with just a few exceptions. Concerning sodium, the histogram shows that most items do not contain a lot of sodium.

Speaking about beef&pork and chicken&fish, a remarkable point is that there is more vitamin C than vitamin A in these products. Values of vitamin A vary from 1 to 10 and of vitamin C from 0 to about 30. However, in McWraps there are far more vitamins than in other items, as these products contain vegetables. There are a little bit more calories from fat in beef&pork than in chicken&fish. Contamination of carbohydrates is pretty the same in both categories.
